AIE Summer Camp

During the summer of 2015 I was an instructor for a summer camp at AIE that tought middle school to high school kids how to make video games in Unity3d. After the introduction and how to set up a project in Unity3d, I noticed that few of the kids were playing a game called agar.io during a break, so I thought I would show them how to make a game like it. It was a win-win because it kept them focused on their game and it had several mechanics that can be easly explained, and appilied to any games they create in the future. First I tought them how to move their character, then how to pickup coins and score points, and finally how which coin they pick up could make their character either grow, or shrink.

8-Hour Game

This is one of my favorite projects. One day my class and I were challenged by our teacher to design, develop, and build a complete game in 8 hours. I decided to make an infinity-runner-style game because they're not too dificult to make, and have endless playtime.
